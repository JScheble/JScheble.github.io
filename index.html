Does Restricting Airbnb Lower Rents? Estimating Effects from NYC’s Local Law 18

Conducted a difference-in-differences analysis of NYC's Local Law 18 — one of the world's strictest short-term rental bans — across 82 zip codes from 2019–2025, correcting for a parallel trends violation caused by differential gentrification rates. Findings show the law acted as a rent stabilizer, reducing growth by ~0.5% per unit of Airbnb intensity, translating to estimated monthly savings of up to $300 in high-density neighborhoods like Williamsburg.

Estimated effects of Marijuana Legalization on Usage Rates

An econometric analysis of U.S. state data using Difference-in-Differences and LASSO regression to evaluate the causal and predictive impact of legalization. Found a ~6% increase in adult usage with no significant change among teens.
